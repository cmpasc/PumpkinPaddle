Background

Gary Kristensen first took up growing pumpkins as a hobby, but soon pushed himself to cultivate a giant pumpkin. After succeeding, he set an even more ambitious goal: carving the massive pumpkin into a personal paddle boat and navigating it down the Columbia River in Oregon. During his maiden voyage in the fall of 2024, he traveled 45.67 miles, earning a Guinness World Record.

Setting a new record

Gary Kristensen wasn’t satisfied with simply holding the Guinness World Record for the longest journey by pumpkin boat. Confident he could go even farther, he set out to grow a pumpkin weighing over 1,200 pounds, timed to mature in May. This was no small feat, requiring a greenhouse, ground heaters, and countless hours of care and attention. After successfully growing his new pumpkin, “Marchflower,” Gary turned his focus back to the river. Over the course of two days of paddling, his second journey concluded on May 24, 2025, covering 58.57 miles (94.26 km) and setting a new Guiness World Record, surpassing his own 2024 record of 45.67 miles.
